20 Minutes or Less - Ch. 1

Being a student is sometimes stressful, especially if you also work part time. Having a healthy diet, or sometimes even just eating enough can be difficult, due to lack of time, money or skill.

But cooking can be a really enjoyable and fun activity once you figure it out. Today we’re learning a cheap and quick, vegetarian recipe for a burrito. Who doesn’t like a burrito? Especially one you can customize to your liking!

You will need:

  • Tortilla wraps
  • Long Grain Rice
  • Chili Kidney Beans
  • Sour cream
  • Vegetables of choice
  • Salt, pepper and other spices (paprika, chilly etc)
  • Optional: Guacamole, Ceddar

Step 1: The Rice

Start by boiling water for the rice. You’ll use about 40g of rice per burrito so ration accordingly. REMA 1000 has 125g portioned rice bags, which is enough for 3 burritos.

Double-rinse the rice, then add it to the boiling water. The standard ratio is 2 cups of water to 1 cup of rice. Boil accordingly to the instructions, or around 15 minutes.

Step 2: Prepping the fillings

While the rice is boiling, empty the beans in a pan and heat them up, or microwave them for a bit.

Take the vegetables of your choice (eg. peppers, iceberg salad, tomato cubes, hot peppers) and chop them up in either strips, cubes or slices. If you’re using peppers, you can cook them in a greased pan until soft.

Step 3: The Assembly

Heat up a flat pan and place the tortilla on it, 10-15 seconds on each side to warm it up.

After the rice is cooked, strain the remaining water and season it to your taste. I like to use this Fajita mix and some extra hot sauce, sometimes mixing in some lemon juice for the zing.

Start filling the burrito. Lay the tortilla on a flat surface and add a layer of 2-3 tablespoons of rice (40g), then flatten it down, 2 tablespoons of beans and 1 tablespoon of vegetables. Top it all off with some drizzled guacamole, sour cream or grated ceddar.

Tip! You can store the remaining ingredients like rice or beans in a sealed container or bag in the fridge for up to 3 days and just assemble in 5 minutes a tasty burrito.

Follow this video to roll the burrito, then grill it on each side to seal it up and enjoy a tasty and filling, easy burrito!

Total: ≈50 dkk for min. 6 servings/ 8,3 dkk per serving
